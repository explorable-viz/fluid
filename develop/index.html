fluid:
functional interactive dataflow

Fluid 0.4 is a new PureScript implementation of Fluid developed by Roly Perera and Minh Nguyen, implementing the ideas described in our POPL 2022 submission, co-authored with Tomas Petricek and Meng Wang. The implementation is currently much slower than the 0.3 TypeScript implementation because of a naive approach to computing environment joins; we will fix this in the forthcoming 0.5 release.

The following images (which will take several seconds to load) are generated by our implementation and provided the raw material for various figures in the POPL paper, as described below. Note that these figures are not intended to be interactive, although d3.js adds tooltips to the bar charts automatically.

Figure 2

The following images were used for Figure 2. They shows how dualising the Galois dependency analysis flips the direction of the Galois connection, by swapping the roles of the upper and lower adjoints. Doing so transforms the analysis from something which determines what we can compute with some selected data into something which determines what we cannot compute without the selected data. This offers a principled basis for an automated brushing and linking feature between two cognate outputs, as shown above. When the user selects part of the output of one (the highlighted bar in the bar chart on the left), we can use backward dependency to compute the needed data, and then the De Morgan dual of the forward dependency to compute the parts of the second output that depend on any of that data (the highlighted points in the line chart on the right).

Figure 1

Fig. 1 was generated using the table and bar chart from Fig. 2 (above), and combining with those with the additional bar chart and table below. (The two bar charts are identical; the second one can be ignored.) The two tables were combined (manually) into the table in the centre of Fig. 1, and highlighting colour for one of the bars/tables was changed from orange to blue. The resulting composite figure shows how different output selections induce different selections on the input.

Figure 13

Finally, the following two images were used for Figure 13 in the paper, and show the four dependency relations. Each figure corresponds to one of the four possible round-trips. The first shows the backward-evaluation Galois connection and how it round-trips with the forward analysis; the second shows the De Morgan dual of the forward analysis and how it round-trips with the De Morgan dual of the backward analysis. These are both described in detail in the paper.
